OBSCYCRA FILE V–013: VAMPIRE

Species Tag: Homo Sanguinarius (unofficial)
Known Aliases: Strigoi, Nosferatu, Jiangshi, Moroi, Upir, The Undead

1. What is a Vampire?

A vampire is an entity—living or undead—that feeds on vital essence, most often human blood. Nearly every culture has some version of the myth. While folklore varies, the core idea remains chillingly consistent: something out there drinks the blood of the living.

"They don’t 'live forever.' They persist. That’s different."

2. Folklore Database

  • Strigoi (Romania): Undead spirit that returns to torment the living.
  • Jiangshi (China): The hopping corpse, drains life-force (qi).
  • Vetala (India): Spirits that possess corpses.
  • Asanbosam (Ghana): Iron-toothed tree vampire.
  • El Chupacabra (Latin America): Animal-like, attacks livestock, drinks blood.
"All cultures. Different forms. Same hunger."

4. Medical Connections

  • Porphyria: Causes sunlight sensitivity, gum recession—linked to vampire traits.
  • Rabies: Aggression, biting, aversion to light/water.
  • Catalepsy: Death-like state—explains buried alive cases.
  • Tuberculosis: Linked to vampire panics in 1800s USA.
"Diseases don’t burn hearts. Belief does."

5. Historical Encounters

  • Arnold Paole (Serbia, 1726): Claimed post-death activity. Body exhumed: fresh blood present.
  • Mercy Brown (Rhode Island, 1892): Body burned post-mortem during TB outbreak.
  • Modern Cases: Sanguinarian communities (real blood-drinkers) exist and are documented.
